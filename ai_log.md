Forced tool_choice rules out extended thinking (the two are incompatible), which is fine for a single structured tool call. First harness run returned the deliberately-vague Boeing question at high confidence; tightened the prompt so confidence reflects how well-specified the question is, not how plausible the guess is, and the rerun returns Boeing at low confidence with notes listing what it guessed (Molina also dropped to medium). All five plans validated on the first attempt.

Entry 5 - Anomaly sidebar and React frontend
Asked: add a pre-computed anomaly backend (three pure-pandas signals plus GET /anomalies) and build the full React/Vite/TypeScript/Tailwind/Recharts single-page app (query input, editable interpretation card, chart and table view, anomaly sidebar), then run both servers and confirm the edit-and-rerun flow works.
Built: anomalies.py computes vendor concentration (one vendor over 50 percent of a SubCategory with at least 1M spend), outlier payments (over 3 sigma above the Category mean and over 10M), and cross-agency vendors (paid by 5 or more agencies), each as an Anomaly model with title/description/suggested_question/severity, top 3 per signal, warmed into a module-level cache by a FastAPI lifespan handler and served at GET /anomalies. Notes: Verified the differentiator end to end - "Top 10 vendors at DSHS" returns Consumer Direct Care ($2.06B) for Social and Health Services, and editing that chip to Health Care Authority and clicking run re-executes via plan_override and returns Molina ($5.25B); the results change.
